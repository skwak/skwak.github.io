So: Some big things have happened in my life. I graduated from [Ada Developers Academy](http://www.adadevelopersacademy.org). I became employed as a Software Engineer I at [Nordstrom](http://www.nordstrom.com) (Remember to buy all your gifts from Nordstrom for the upcoming holidays ;-)).

But one thing that hasn't changed is the continuous quest of learning. This is part of what drew me to software development in the first place and what makes it such an exciting, creative endeavor. It is also what makes it so hard and sometimes excruciatingly frustrating.

I have a bunch of goals of things I want to learn. React, Clojure, Node, Express, design patterns, computer science fundamentals are some of them. I am not sure I will get to React or Clojure anytime soon, but I just want to share to you (and to my future self, remembering the things past and searching for lost time) some of the things I've been doing recently to keep up this endless learning cycle. Why? To [check myself](https://youtu.be/5aAbOgdbTbM), my patterns, and my inclinations, and also to chart my progress.

* [Code School](http://www.codeschool.com)
Through the generosity of [Strange Loop](http://www.thestrangeloop.com), I have a one-year subscription to Code School. I've gone through the SQL path, just finished the Node.js path, and am planning on doing various Ruby (I want to go back to my coding roots!) and JavaScript paths.

* [NODESCHOOL](http://nodeschool.io/)
I've done the [Learn You Node](https://github.com/workshopper/learnyounode) workshopper,
and the "Juggling Async" problem [slayed me!](https://github.com/skwak/learnyounode-answers) and also was that little lightbulb in my head that went off.

* Beautiful books
As you may know, I like design. I like nice-looking things. I also like things that aren't so nice-looking but are interesting, clever, witty, maybe ironic. My past experience working in the art world has taught me that interesting, clever, or aesthetically-pleasing experiences create value in nuanced ways that bring small, important daily joys and meanings <-- sounds like a LinkedIn bio of a UX professional. Ugh! But it's true. I'm being sincere.

I also like books a lot. I like to read and write and have always been fond of poetry, the experimental, classic narratives, etc.

So it's always a little painful to me to browse programming books and see the sea of ugly, un-interesting, not-very-clever, and boring programming books out there. But there are some exceptions out there, and I'm slowly discovering a few of them as time goes by. One of them is [Eloquent JavaScript](http://eloquentjavascript.net/), which I had read bits and pieces of during Ada but didn't start looking at it for real REAL until a few months ago.

The other book I'm reading (and I just started reading it because it arrived in the mail today) is *The Little Schemer*, another gem heard about through [Strange Loop](http://www.thestrangeloop.com).

![holding The Little Schemer](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/CUW-8IlUsAA6tD6.jpg:large)

So, basically, Node, JavaScript, and functional programming is in my horizon. A nice way to end 2015, and a nice way to begin 2016.
